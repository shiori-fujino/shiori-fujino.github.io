Medication Journal 2

2021.06.23 paliperidone invega seroquel

It’s been half a year after I wrote Medication journal (1) and the medication still is wearing off. I, at last, started the magic day of the month for the first time in 6 months, but I still lactate only ever so slightly when I squeeze my blueberries hard enough.

I stumbled upon watching a documentary1 and tv series2 about trauma recently. It’s a huge progress that I could finish an hour of documentary film and 10 episodes of tv series. When I just got the Invega, I hardly ever was interested in anything. Books, movies, songs… literally nothing. I’m a little shy to say this but these days, I like Justin Bieber’s song. lol “Peaches” is beautiful.

I start to focus on strange things (I’m seeing numbers like 747, 777, 797) and maybe subtly delusional but ok, I prefer being a psycho to a numb-brainer.

I wrote numerous times about my issues and problems previously, so you’d know well how messed up I am if you’ve been following my blogs, but let me briefly demonstrate me again: Broken family, Seeking approval, Codependent relationships, Low self-esteem, therefore lack of self-love, and so on… Like everyone else, my life stuck in snowballed trauma. Everyone lives with trauma in their piggyback. Some might be raised in a broken family, some got bullied in school, some has been rejected by many people, some might always sense the worthlessness, some might have been through everything above like me.

Working as a sex worker definitely cured some problems3 by getting enough daily dose of attention and praises(that I never had in my entire life). Seeing various types of people naked and observe them widen my perspective about life. I always had too much in my head. Someday I decided to write all those mingled thoughts deep inside of me and It helped a lot. It made me realise what the great cause of all problems was. It’s ok if I can’t change what happened in the past, at least I know where it comes from when I’m about to lose my sanity. So… there are at least 2 things that I think I’ve accomplished in my life.

On the other hand, professional help, especially pharmaceutical help wasn’t helpful at all. I get it, the world needs more doctors and nurses to treat sick people but we only have limited time and material so… meds are easier for everyone. But it won’t work for everyone. I wish I knew what Paliperodone, or Seroquel can do to me. I figured too late but I hope someone can get a clue from what I write. If a doctor prescribes you paliperidone(Invega), run, run fast until they can’t find you!!! There are several posts on Reddit about paliperidone(Invega) so you might check that out too. I don’t know, my advice from my own experience not going to work for everyone, and some people have to take meds no matter what. If you do or don’t, the best advice from me: Hang in there, days are coming.4

It’s almost been a year since I became a qualified psycho. Life goes on. I’ll write meds journal (3) when I can stop taking Seroquel5, so when I am an entirely drug-free person.
